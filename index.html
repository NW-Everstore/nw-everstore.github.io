Whitepaper 01 - Abstract - Feb 15th, 2016

Since the advent of Bitcoin the ecosystem has been lacking a solution to store arbitrary forms of data actually within the underlying blockchain. Most approaches are limited to the storing of anchored data-hashes such as those provided by Factom and Tierion, with the actual data then stored off-chain.

By employing a distributed database as a protocol, entities would be able to read or write structured data directly into or out of a compatible blockchain - removing the need to store any of that information within their own siloed databases. Applications could operate freely without the need to rely on a centralised version of the truth; confident that the data they have received is from an immutable source.

The ability to store complete data schemas and their corresponding data is achieved by structuring the limited storage space available from within the OP_RETURN functionality of outgoing transactions across multiple nodes. These are purposely sent from specifically derived HD (Hierarchical Deterministic) BIP32 addresses - where each branch of derived addresses can represent a certain series of actions and (or) defining properties.
